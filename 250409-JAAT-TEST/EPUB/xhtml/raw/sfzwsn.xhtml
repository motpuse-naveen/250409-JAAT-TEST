Linking It All Together

Just as the human body is linked together by the kinetic chain, let us link what we learned in Chapter 6. A good way to do that is to revisit the questions from the beginning of the chapter to inspire discussion with your peers and instructor. We can also link to current evidence to further explore the questions.

To link to generalized discussion points online, go to booksofdiscovery.com, “For Students.”

  • Can you identify all of the muscles that originate from the pelvic bones that assist in movement of the knee?
    • Create a list and utilize Trail Guide to the Body to confirm your selections. 
      • To link to an example of current evidence to support a discussion, go to: 

Marjorie V. Launico, Margaret A. Sinkler, and Shivajee V. Nallamothu, “Anatomy, Bony Pelvis and Lower Limb, Femoral Muscles,” StatPearls, updated September 20, 2022, https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK500008/.

  •  Why is pseudo-sciatica/piriformis syndrome more common in runners?
    • There are various factors that make runners more susceptible to this medical condition, but, generally, the piriformis will spasm due to overuse and fatigue.
      • To link to an example of current evidence to support a discussion, go to: 

Brandon L. Hicks, Jason C. Lam, and Matthew Varacallo, “Piriformis Syndrome,” StatPearls, updated September 4, 2022, https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK448172/.

  • Why do injuries to the hip and pelvis have such a debilitating impact on daily activities?
    • While there many possible answers to this question, the most basic answer is this:
      • This is a weight-bearing region of the body; therefore, it is constantly under some degree of stress whether we are at rest or moving, sitting, or standing, laughing or crying.
      • To link to an example of current evidence to support a discussion, go to: 

Renan Alves Resende et al., “Does Trunk and Hip Muscles Strength Predict Performance during a Core Stability Test?” Revista Brasileira de Fisioterapia (Brazilian Journal of Physical Therapy) 24, no. 4 (July–August 2020): 318–24, https://doi.org/10.1016/j.bjpt.2019.03.001.
